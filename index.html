Get to know us...

Leo Club of Colombo Downtown Alliance is a fresh, young energetic Leo club with young leaders who would love to serve the society through ‘Leadership, Experience, Opportunity’ and Leo club of Colombo Downtown Alliance encourage youth to develop their leadership qualities by participating in social service activities.

The Leo Club of Colombo Downtown Alliance was chartered on 18th of August 2020 by the leadership of Leo Lion Nethmi Thathsarani as the Club Charter President of the club. Since 2020, by 3 young strong club presidents led the club up to now and nowadays, Downtown is rising as one of the best Leo clubs of Leo District 306B2.

Leo Club of Colombo Downtown Alliance currently has 130+ active members and completed various projects ‘DT We Inspire, Downtown Wenuwen Api, Today IS, Sustainable Leadership Development, Checkmate, DT Eduhub, Yuthukama, Electic, Ashirwada, Diabetic Pioneer’ likewise that covers almost every side of the society such as Health Care, Elder Care, Children, Differently Abled, Literacy & Education, and Youth Development.

During this short term Downtown not only provided excellent community service, but also helped many youngsters to find their aptitudes during this term. Also anyone who would like to serve the society and built up their selves are welcomed to our family.

Joining the Leo Club provides opportunities for members to develop their leadership skills through participating in various community service projects, fundraising activities and planning events. ELECTRIC

Project for Sustainable Development Goals

The targeted benefiting community for this initiative are Leos, Leo Lions & Lions in district 306 B2 and other multiple districts as well as people in Sri Lanka. With the aim of benefiting approximately 201 members globally, the initiative also aims to benefit the Sri Lankan ecosystem and its people. The initiative encourages Leo leaders to share their thoughts and ideas with the public, thereby promoting constructive thinking and building strong bonds with fellow Leo leaders and those in need of volunteering services.

The Global Village will also provide an opportunity for young people to engage with global volunteers and expand international connections. This will not only benefit the youth, but also contribute to the development of the tourism industry. Leos will have the chance to work with non-Leo organizations such as SUNFO, allowing them to better understand other volunteering members and serve the community with a greater impact in the future.

Be My Eyes

Project for Vision

Joint Project w/Leo Club of Colombo Plaza & Lions Club of Colombo Somerset

The project aims to improve services for blind and visually impaired children by raising awareness on how to help and letting others know their unique perspective. It prioritizes aid to underserved populations who lack access to eye care, education, and rehabilitation due to economic, social, or geographic barriers. By delivering high-quality services and improving existing infrastructure, the project seeks to create sustainable eye care systems.

The target community includes the general public, school children, and those with impaired vision. In Sri Lanka, 8.7% of the population above five years old lives with some form of disability, including 996,939 who are visually impaired or blind. The Lions Club of Colombo Eye Care has been identified as a partner to provide expertise and aid in educating the public on eye health, symptom identification, eye exams, and assisting those with impaired vision. The goal is to improve the quality of life for visually impaired individuals in Sri Lanka.

Diabetic Pioneer

Project for Research and Development & Diabetes

Diabetic Pioneer is an initiative launched by the Leos of Combo Downtown Alliance-District 306 B2 to reduce the prevalence of diabetes among the younger generation by promoting better health habits. The project is aimed at high-risk individuals aged 18 to 65 and is intended to improve the public's awareness of diabetes, which is often underestimated. The goal is to create sustainable solutions for the healthcare system and improve the quality of life for future generations.

The project is based on the identified community need for diabetes education, as the prevalence of diabetes has increased globally and is expected to reach 152 million by 2045. The awareness of diabetes was found to be limited to diabetic awareness month, with a lack of targeting of the younger generation. To address this issue, the project will collect frequently asked questions about diabetes and provide answers from doctors in an effort to educate the community and create a continuous flow of knowledge on this topic.
